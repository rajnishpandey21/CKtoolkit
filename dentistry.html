NEET UG for BDS Admission: An In-Depth Guide

Exam Structure and Content

The NEET UG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test-Undergraduate) exam serves as the gateway to BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) programs across India. It is a single-day, pen-and-paper-based test administered by the National Testing Agency (NTA). The exam consists of four sections:

  • Physics: 45 questions covering topics from the 11th and 12th standard syllabus.
  • Chemistry: 45 questions based on the 11th and 12th standard Chemistry curriculum.
  • Biology (Botany and Zoology): 90 questions (45 each for Botany and Zoology) from the 11th and 12th standard syllabus.
  • General Knowledge and Aptitude: 10 questions assessing general awareness and aptitude.

Duration: 3 hours

Skills Required: Efficient time management and focused problem-solving abilities.

Scoring and Marking Scheme

  • Total Marks: 720
  • Correct Answer: +4 marks
  • Incorrect Answer: -1 mark (negative marking)
  • Unanswered Questions: No marks awarded or deducted

Eligibility Criteria

  • Educational Qualification: 12th standard or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English as compulsory subjects.
  • Minimum Marks: 50% aggregate in PCB (40% for SC/ST/OBC candidates).
  • Age Limit: Minimum 17 years old as of December 31st of the year of admission.
