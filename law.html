CLAT (Common Law Admission Test) for BBA LL.B. (Hons.)

Introduction

The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the Consortium of National Law Universities (NLUs) for admission to undergraduate and postgraduate law programs in India. CLAT is a two-hour online test that assesses the candidate's knowledge of legal aptitude, logical reasoning, English language, current affairs, and general knowledge.

Structure of the Exam

CLAT is divided into five sections:

  • English Language: 40 questions
  • Current Affairs, Including General Knowledge: 50 questions
  • Legal Reasoning: 50 questions
  • Logical Reasoning: 40 questions
  • Quantitative Techniques: 20 questions

Paper Pattern

  • The CLAT question paper consists of 150 multiple-choice questions.
  • Each question carries one mark.
  • There is no negative marking for incorrect answers.
  • The medium of the exam is English.

Marking Scheme

  • Each correct answer is awarded one mark.
  • There is no negative marking for incorrect answers.
  • The total marks for the CLAT exam are 150.

AILET for B.Com LL.B. (Hons.)

Introduction

The All India Law Entrance Test (AILET) is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Law University, Delhi (NLU Delhi) for admission to its undergraduate law programs, including the B.Com LL.B. (Hons.) program.

Structure of the Exam

The AILET for B.Com LL.B. (Hons.) consists of two parts:

  • Part I: Objective-type questions from:
    • English Language
    • Current Affairs and General Knowledge
    • Legal Aptitude
    • Logical Reasoning
  • Part II: Subjective-type questions from:
    • Elementary Mathematics
    • Economics

Paper Pattern

Part I:

  • Number of questions: 150
  • Time duration: 90 minutes
  • Marking scheme: +1 mark for each correct answer; -0.25 marks for each incorrect answer

Part II:

  • Number of questions: 5
  • Time duration: 60 minutes
  • Marking scheme: 20 marks for each question

Marking Scheme

Part I:

  • Correct answer: +1 mark
  • Incorrect answer: -0.25 marks
  • Unanswered question: 0 marks

Part II:

  • Each question carries 20 marks

LSAT India for BA LL.B. (Hons.)

Introduction

The Law School Admission Test (LSAT) India is a standardized test administered by Pearson VUE for admissions to undergraduate law programs in India.

Structure of the Exam

The LSAT—India is a two-hour, multiple-choice test divided into four 35-minute sections:

  • Analytical Reasoning: 24 questions
  • Logical Reasoning: 26 questions
  • Reading Comprehension: 28 questions
  • Verbal Reasoning: 20 questions

Paper Pattern

  • The test is conducted in English.
  • All questions are multiple-choice, with four answer options.
  • There is no penalty for guessing.

Marking Scheme

  • Each question is worth one point.
  • There is no negative marking.
  • The total score ranges from 44 to 180.

SLAT (Symbiosis Law Admission Test) for BA LL.B. (Hons.)

Exam Structure

SLAT is a three-hour, objective-type entrance exam conducted by Symbiosis International (Deemed University) for admission to its five-year BA LL.B. (Hons.) program across its campuses.

Exam Sections

  • Logical Reasoning
  • Legal Reasoning
  • Analytical Reasoning
  • Reading Comprehension

Paper Pattern

  • Total number of questions: 150
  • Total marks: 150
  • Duration of the exam: 3 hours
  • Marking scheme: +1 mark for each correct answer, no negative marking

Colleges Accepting SLAT for BA LL.B. (Hons.)

  • Symbiosis Law School, Pune
  • Symbiosis Law School, Hyderabad
  • Symbiosis Law School, Noida
  • Symbiosis Law School, Nagpur
  • Symbiosis Law School, Bengaluru
  • Symbiosis Law School, Mumbai
  • Symbiosis Law School, Chennai

Other Information

  • SLAT is conducted once a year, usually in May.
  • The application process for SLAT typically begins in December.
  • Candidates must have a bachelor's degree or equivalent qualification in any discipline from a recognized university to be eligible for SLAT.
  • The selection process for SLAT includes the written exam, followed by a personal interview and group discussion for shortlisted candidates.
  • SLAT is a highly competitive exam, and admission to Symbiosis Law School is based on merit.
