JEE (Mains, Advanced)

Introduction

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to various engineering and architecture courses in India. The JEE is conducted in two stages: JEE Main and JEE Advanced.

Format

  • JEE Main is a computer-based test (CBT) conducted in two sessions: January and April.
  • JEE Advanced is a paper-based test (PBT) conducted in May.
  • Both JEE Main and JEE Advanced consist of three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Eligibility

  • Candidates who have passed Class 12 or an equivalent examination with at least 75% marks in aggregate (65% for SC/ST candidates) in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are eligible to appear for JEE Main.
  • Candidates who have secured a top 2,50,000 rank in JEE Main are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.

Scope

  • JEE Main scores are accepted by various engineering and architecture colleges across India for admission to undergraduate programs.
  • JEE Advanced scores are accepted by the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), the National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and other premier engineering colleges for admission to undergraduate programs.

BITSAT (Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test)

Introduction

The Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test (BITSAT) is a national-level entrance examination conducted by BITS Pilani for admissions into its Integrated First-Degree programs, including:

  • Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.)
  • Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm.)
  • Master of Science (M.Sc.)

BITS Pilani has campuses in Pilani, Goa, Hyderabad, and Dubai, and BITSAT scores are accepted across all these campuses.

Exam Structure and Pattern

BITSAT is a computer-based test (CBT) with a duration of 3 hours. The question paper comprises multiple-choice questions (MCQs) divided into four parts:

  • Part I: Physics – 30 questions
  • Part II: Chemistry – 30 questions
  • Part III:
    • English Proficiency – 10 questions
    • Logical Reasoning – 20 questions
  • Part IV: Mathematics/Biology (depending on the program) – 40 questions

The total number of questions is 130, and the maximum marks are 390. Each correct answer awards 3 marks, while each incorrect answer results in a deduction of 1 mark. Unattempted questions do not affect the score.

Additional Question Feature: If a candidate answers all 130 questions before the allotted time, they have the option to attempt an additional 12 questions (four each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology), allowing for the possibility of scoring higher.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for BITSAT, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • For all programs except B.Pharm.: Candidates should have passed the 12th examination from a recognized Central or State board or its equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • For B.Pharm.: Candidates should have passed the 12th examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. However, candidates with Mathematics are also eligible to apply.
  • Minimum Marks:
    • A minimum aggregate of 75% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology in the 12th examination.
    • At least 60% marks in each of the subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology.
  • Year of Passing: Candidates appearing for the 12th examination in the current year or those who have passed in the previous year are eligible to apply.
  • Proficiency in English: Adequate proficiency in English is required.
  • Direct Admission: First-rank holders of all central and state boards are offered direct admission to their preferred program, irrespective of their BITSAT score, provided they meet the above eligibility criteria.

Participating Institutes

BITSAT scores are primarily accepted by the following BITS campuses:

  • BITS Pilani, Pilani Campus: The flagship campus known for its legacy and academic excellence.
  • BITS Pilani, K.K. Birla Goa Campus: Established in 2004, offering a picturesque environment and modern infrastructure.
  • BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus: Inaugurated in 2008, known for its state-of-the-art facilities and research initiatives.
  • BITS Pilani, Dubai Campus: Catering to international students, offering diverse programs in engineering and sciences.
  • NIIT University, Neemrana: Accepts BITSAT scores for admission into its B.Tech programs, providing candidates with broader options.

Additional Information

  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for BITSAT online through the official BITS admission website. The application window typically opens in January and closes in April.
  • Exam Sessions: BITSAT is conducted in multiple sessions, allowing candidates to choose their preferred test date and center based on availability.

Preparation Tips

  • Familiarize yourself with the exam pattern and syllabus.
  • Practice with previous years' question papers and mock tests.
  • Focus on time management and accuracy.
  • Strengthen fundamental concepts in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, and English Proficiency & Logical Reasoning.

For detailed information, including updates on application dates, exam schedules, and counseling procedures, candidates are advised to regularly visit the official BITS admission website.

VITEEE (VIT Engineering Entrance Examination)

Introduction

The VIT Engineering Entrance Examination (VITEEE) is a national-level entrance test conducted by the Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) for admissions into its Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) programs across various campuses. The exam evaluates candidates' proficiency in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, Aptitude, and English.

Exam Structure and Pattern

VITEEE is a computer-based test (CBT) with a duration of 2 hours and 30 minutes. The question paper comprises multiple-choice questions (MCQs) divided into the following sections:

  • Physics: 35 questions
  • Chemistry: 35 questions
  • Mathematics/Biology: 40 questions
  • Aptitude: 10 questions
  • English: 5 questions

In total, there are 125 questions, each carrying 1 mark, making the maximum score 125 marks. Notably, there is no negative marking for incorrect answers.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for VITEEE, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Applicants should be residents or non-resident Indians. Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) are also eligible.
  • Age Limit: Candidates must be born on or after July 1, 2003.
  • Educational Qualification:
    • Must have completed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology as mandatory subjects.
    • Should secure an aggregate of 60% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology in the qualifying examination.
    • For candidates belonging to SC/ST categories, the minimum aggregate marks required is 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology.

Participating Institutes

VITEEE scores are primarily accepted by the following VIT campuses:

  • Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT), Vellore: The main campus offering a wide range of engineering disciplines.
  • VIT Chennai: Known for its modern infrastructure and industry collaborations.
  • VIT-AP University, Amaravati: Offers innovative programs with a focus on research and development.
  • VIT Bhopal University: Emphasizes a technology-driven curriculum with state-of-the-art facilities.

These campuses offer various B.Tech specializations, including but not limited to Computer Science and Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Civil Engineering.

Additional Information

  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for VITEEE online through the official VIT website. The application window typically opens in November and closes in March.
  • Exam Sessions: VITEEE is conducted in multiple sessions, and candidates can choose their preferred test date and center based on availability.

Preparation Tips

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly.
  • Practice with previous years' question papers and take mock tests to assess your preparation.
  • Focus on time management and accuracy during practice sessions.
  • Strengthen your fundamentals in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, and enhance your Aptitude and English skills.

For detailed information, including updates on application dates, exam schedules, and counseling procedures, candidates are advised to regularly visit the official VIT website.

SRMJEEE (SRM Joint Engineering Entrance Examination)

Introduction

The SRM Joint Engineering Entrance Examination (SRMJEEE) is a national-level entrance test conducted by SRM Institute of Science and Technology (SRMIST) for admissions into its Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) and Integrated M.Tech programs across various campuses. The exam assesses candidates' knowledge in subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, English, and Aptitude.

Exam Structure and Pattern

SRMJEEE is a computer-based test (CBT) with a duration of 2 hours and 30 minutes. The question paper comprises multiple-choice questions (MCQs) divided into the following sections:

  • Physics: 35 questions
  • Chemistry: 35 questions
  • Mathematics/Biology: 40 questions
  • English: 5 questions
  • Aptitude: 10 questions

In total, there are 125 questions, each carrying 1 mark, making the maximum score 125 marks. Notably, there is no negative marking for incorrect answers.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for SRMJEEE, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Applicants should be residents or non-resident Indians. Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) are also eligible.
  • Age Limit: Candidates must be born between July 1, 2003, and July 1, 2008.
  • Educational Qualification:
    • Must have completed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology as mandatory subjects.
    • Should secure a minimum aggregate of 60% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology in the qualifying examination.
  • Direct Admission: SRMIST offers direct admission and scholarships to first-rank holders of all central and state boards in India, top 10,000 IIT JEE rankers, top rankers from each district of Tamil Nadu, and exemplary sportspersons at the national and international levels.

Participating Institutes

SRMJEEE scores are accepted by the following SRM campuses:

  • SRM Institute of Science and Technology (SRMIST), Kattankulathur: The main campus in Tamil Nadu, offering a wide range of engineering disciplines.
  • SRMIST, Ramapuram Campus: Located in Chennai, known for its modern infrastructure and academic excellence.
  • SRMIST, Vadapalani Campus: Also in Chennai, focusing on industry-oriented education.
  • SRMIST, Delhi-NCR Campus, Ghaziabad: Catering to students in the northern region with diverse engineering programs.
  • SRM University, Sonepat (Haryana): Emphasizes research and innovation in engineering education.
  • SRM University, Andhra Pradesh: Offers interdisciplinary engineering programs with a global perspective.

These campuses provide a variety of B.Tech specializations, including Computer Science and Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Civil Engineering, and more.

Additional Information

  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for SRMJEEE online through the official SRMIST website. The application window for Phase I is open from November 12, 2024, to April 16, 2025.
  • Exam Mode: The examination is conducted in Remote Proctored Online Mode (RPOM), allowing candidates to take the test from their homes while being monitored remotely.

Preparation Tips

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly.
  • Practice with previous years' question papers and take mock tests to assess your preparation.
  • Focus on time management and accuracy during practice sessions.
  • Strengthen your fundamentals in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, and enhance your English and Aptitude skills.

For detailed information, including updates on application dates, exam schedules, and counseling procedures, candidates are advised to regularly visit the official SRMIST website.

MET (Manipal Entrance Test)

Introduction

The Manipal Entrance Test (MET) is a university-level examination conducted by the Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE) for admissions into various undergraduate and postgraduate programs, notably the Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) courses across its campuses. The exam evaluates candidates' proficiency in subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, English, and Aptitude.

Exam Structure and Pattern

MET is a computer-based test (CBT) with a duration of 2 hours. The question paper comprises multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and numerical answer type (NAT) questions, divided into the following sections:

  • Physics: 10 MCQs & 5 NAT
  • Chemistry: 10 MCQs & 5 NAT
  • Mathematics: 15 MCQs & 5 NAT
  • English: 10 MCQs

In total, there are 60 questions, each carrying 4 marks, making the maximum score 240 marks.

Marking Scheme:

  • For MCQs, each correct answer awards 4 marks, while each incorrect answer results in a deduction of 1 mark.
  • For NAT questions, 4 marks are awarded for correct answers, with no negative marking for incorrect responses.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for MET, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Applicants should be Indian citizens.
  • Educational Qualification:
    • Must have completed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination from a recognized board with Physics, Mathematics, and English as compulsory subjects, along with Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/any technical vocational subject as optional.
    • Should secure a minimum aggregate of 50% marks in Physics, Mathematics, and any one of the optional subjects.
  • Age Limit: There is no age limit specified for the examination.

Participating Institutes

MET scores are accepted by the following MAHE constituent institutions:

  • Manipal Institute of Technology (MIT), Manipal: The main campus offering a wide range of engineering disciplines.
  • Manipal University Jaipur (MUJ): Known for its modern infrastructure and academic excellence.
  • Sikkim Manipal Institute of Technology (SMIT), Sikkim: Catering to students in the northeastern region with diverse engineering programs.
  • MIT Bengaluru: A newly established campus focusing on innovative engineering education.

These campuses collectively offer various B.Tech specializations, including Computer Science and Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Civil Engineering, and more.

Additional Information

  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for MET online through the official MAHE website. The application process for Phase 1 is ongoing, with the last date to apply being March 15, 2025.
  • Slot Booking: Registered candidates must complete the slot booking process to select their preferred exam date and time. Slots are allocated on a first-come, first-served basis, subject to availability.

Preparation Tips

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly.
  • Practice with previous years' question papers and take mock tests to assess your preparation.
  • Focus on time management and accuracy during practice sessions.
  • Strengthen your fundamentals in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and enhance your English and Aptitude skills.

For detailed information, including updates on application dates, exam schedules, and counseling procedures, candidates are advised to regularly visit the official MAHE website.

MHT CET (Maharashtra Common Entrance Test)

Introduction

The Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET) is a state-level examination conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, for admissions into undergraduate programs in engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture within the state. The exam is divided into two groups based on the subjects chosen by the candidates:

  • PCM: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
  • PCB: Physics, Chemistry, and Biology

Exam Structure and Pattern

MHT CET is a computer-based test (CBT) with a total duration of 180 minutes (3 hours). The exam is divided into two sections, each lasting 90 minutes:

  • Section I: Physics and Chemistry
  • Section II: Mathematics (for PCM group) or Biology (for PCB group)

Detailed Exam Pattern

Group Subject Number of Questions Marks per Question Total Marks
PCM Physics 50 1 50
Chemistry 50 1 50
Mathematics 50 2 100
PCB Physics 50 1 50
Chemistry 50 1 50
Biology 100 1 100

In the PCM group, the total number of questions is 150, with a maximum score of 200 marks. In the PCB group, there are 200 questions, also totaling 200 marks. Notably, there is no negative marking for incorrect answers.

Syllabus Distribution

The MHT CET syllabus is based on the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education curriculum. The distribution of questions is as follows:

  • Class 11 Syllabus: Approximately 20% of the questions
  • Class 12 Syllabus: Approximately 80% of the questions

This distribution applies to all subjects in both PCM and PCB groups.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for MHT CET, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Indian citizens
  • Educational Qualification:
    • For Engineering and Technology Programs (PCM Group):
      • Must have passed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination with Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/Technical/Vocational subjects.
      • Should have secured a minimum of 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved category candidates) in the above subjects taken together.
    • For Pharmacy Programs (PCB Group):
      • Must have passed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics.
      • Should have secured a minimum of 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved category candidates) in the above subjects taken together.

MHT CET scores are accepted by various institutions across Maharashtra, including:

  • Dwarkadas J. Sanghvi College of Engineering, Mumbai
  • Vishwakarma Institute of Technology, Pune
  • Pune Institute of Computer Technology (PICT), Pune

  • MIT World Peace University, Pune

KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test)

Introduction

The Karnataka Common Entrance Test (KCET) is a state-level examination conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) for admissions into various undergraduate programs, including Engineering, Pharmacy, Agriculture, and Veterinary courses within Karnataka. The exam evaluates candidates' proficiency in subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology.

Exam Structure and Pattern

KCET is an offline, pen-and-paper-based test comprising multiple-choice questions (MCQs). The examination is divided into four sessions, each lasting 1 hour and 20 minutes, conducted over two days. The subject-wise distribution is as follows:

Day 1:

  • Biology: 60 questions
  • Mathematics: 60 questions

Day 2:

  • Physics: 60 questions
  • Chemistry: 60 questions

Each correct answer awards 1 mark, and there is no negative marking for incorrect responses. The total number of questions is 240, with a maximum score of 240 marks.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for KCET, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Applicants should be Indian citizens.
  • Educational Qualification:
    • For Engineering Courses:
      • Must have passed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects, along with Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/Electronics/Computer Science as optional subjects.
      • Should have secured a minimum of 45% aggregate marks (40% for reserved categories) in the above subjects.
    • For Pharmacy Courses:
      • Must have passed or be appearing for the 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics.
      • Should have secured a minimum of 45% aggregate marks (40% for reserved categories) in the above subjects.
  • There is no specified age limit for KCET applicants.

Participating Institutes

KCET scores are accepted by numerous institutions across Karnataka. TNEA (Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions)

Introduction

The Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) is a centralized counseling process conducted by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE), Tamil Nadu, for admission into undergraduate engineering programs across various colleges in the state. Unlike traditional entrance examinations, TNEA does not involve a separate exam; instead, admissions are based on the marks obtained in the Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) examinations.

Eligibility Criteria

To participate in TNEA, candidates must meet the following eligibility requirements:

  • Nativity: Candidates should be natives of Tamil Nadu. Those who have completed their 8th to 12th grades in Tamil Nadu are eligible. Children of All India Service Tamil Nadu cadre officers are also eligible.
  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must have passed the HSC (10+2) or an equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects.
  • Minimum Marks Requirement:
    • General Category: A minimum average of 45% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
    • Backward Classes including Backward Class Muslim: A minimum average of 40%.
    • MBC & DNC: A minimum average of 40%.
    • SC/SCA/ST: A minimum average of 40%.
  • Age Limit: There is no specific age limit for most engineering courses. However, for Marine Engineering, candidates should not be older than 25 years.

Admission Process

  • Application: Candidates must register online through the official TNEA portal during the application window.
  • Random Number Assignment: After successful registration, a unique random number is assigned to each candidate, which is used to break ties during the ranking process.
  • Rank List Publication: A merit list is prepared based on the normalized marks of candidates in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Counseling: Candidates are called for counseling based on their ranks, where they can choose their preferred courses and colleges.

Participating Colleges

TNEA facilitates admissions to numerous engineering colleges across Tamil Nadu. Some of the prominent institutions accepting TNEA admissions include:

  • College of Engineering, Anna University, Chennai: One of the oldest and most prestigious engineering institutions in India, offering a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs.
  • PSG College of Technology, Coimbatore: Known for its strong industry connections and emphasis on research and development.
  • Coimbatore Institute of Technology, Coimbatore: Offers quality education with a focus on practical skills and innovation.
  • SSN College of Engineering, Chennai: A premier institution with state-of-the-art facilities and a strong emphasis on research.
  • Thiagarajar College of Engineering, Madurai: Renowned for its academic excellence and vibrant campus life.
  • Kumaraguru College of Technology, Coimbatore: Offers diverse engineering programs with a focus on holistic development.
  • Kongu Engineering College, Erode: Known for its excellent infrastructure and placement opportunities.
  • Bannari Amman Institute of Technology, Sathyamangalam: Provides a conducive learning environment with modern amenities.
  • Velammal Engineering College, Chennai: Emphasizes discipline and quality education with good placement records.
  • St. Josephs College of Engineering, Chennai: Offers a range of engineering courses with a focus on research and development.

For a comprehensive list of participating colleges, candidates are advised to refer to the official TNEA website or the latest counseling brochure.

Additional Information

  • Reservation Policy: TNEA follows the Tamil Nadu government's reservation policy, allocating seats for various categories such as OBC, SC, ST, and others.
  • Counseling Fees: Candidates are required to pay a counseling fee, which varies based on the category and the number of choices filled.
  • Document Verification: During counseling, candidates must produce original documents for verification, including mark sheets, community certificates, and nativity certificates, if applicable.

For further details, including updates on the application process, counseling schedule, and seat allotment, candidates should regularly visit the official TNEA website.

WBJEE (West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination)

Introduction

The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination (WBJEE) is a state-level entrance test conducted by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB) for admission into undergraduate courses in engineering, technology, architecture, and pharmacy across various institutions in West Bengal.

Exam Structure

The WBJEE comprises two papers:

  • Paper I: Mathematics
  • Paper II: Physics and Chemistry (combined)

Question Types

Both papers feature multiple-choice questions (MCQs) divided into three categories:

  • Category 1: Single correct answer; each question carries 1 mark with a penalty of 0.25 marks for incorrect answers.
  • Category 2: Single correct answer; each question carries 2 marks with a penalty of 0.5 marks for incorrect answers.
  • Category 3: One or more correct answers; each question carries 2 marks. For partially correct responses, marks are awarded based on the number of correct options chosen divided by the total number of correct options. There is no negative marking in this category.

Exam Pattern

Subject Category 1 Questions Category 2 Questions Category 3 Questions Total Questions Total Marks
Mathematics 50 15 10 75 100
Physics 30 5 5 40 50
Chemistry 30 5 5 40 50

Maximum Marks

The total marks for the WBJEE are 200, distributed as follows:

  • Mathematics: 100 marks
  • Physics: 50 marks
  • Chemistry: 50 marks

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for WBJEE, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Candidates must be Indian citizens.
  • Age Limit: Applicants should be at least 17 years old as of December 31 of the examination year. There is no upper age limit, except for the Marine Engineering course, which has an upper age limit of 25 years.
  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects, along with Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/Computer Science/Computer Application as the third subject.
  • Minimum Marks Requirement:
    • General Category: A minimum aggregate of 45% in the three subjects mentioned above.
    • Reserved Categories (SC/ST/OBC-A/OBC-B/PwD): A minimum aggregate of 40%.
  • English Proficiency: Candidates must have secured at least 30% marks in English in their 10+2 examination.

Participating Colleges

WBJEE scores are accepted by numerous government and private institutions in West Bengal. Some of the prominent colleges include:

  • Jadavpur University, Kolkata: Renowned for its engineering programs and consistently ranked among the top engineering institutions in India.
  • Maulana Abul Kalam Azad University of Technology (MAKAUT), Kolkata: Offers a variety of undergraduate and postgraduate engineering courses.
  • Institute of Engineering and Management (IEM), Kolkata: A leading private engineering college known for its excellent placement records.
  • Heritage Institute of Technology, Kolkata: Offers diverse engineering disciplines with modern infrastructure and facilities.

The University of Petroleum and Energy Studies Engineering Aptitude Test (UPES-EAT) is a pivotal national-level entrance exam for aspiring engineers and technologists. Conducted by the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Dehradun, this exam serves as the entry point for a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in the fields of engineering and technology.

Exam Structure and Syllabus

The UPES-EAT exam is designed to assess a candidate's proficiency in core subjects essential for engineering and technology studies. It comprises three sections:

Section 1: Mathematics

  • Algebra: Quadratic equations, complex numbers, permutations and combinations, binomial theorem, matrices and determinants
  • Calculus: Limits, continuity, differentiation, integration, differential equations
  • Coordinate Geometry: Straight lines, circles, conic sections, 3D geometry, vectors

Section 2: Physics

  • Mechanics: Kinematics, laws of motion, work, energy, power, rotational motion, gravitation
  • Electromagnetism: Electrostatics, current electricity, magnetic effects of current, electromagnetic induction
  • Optics: Ray optics, wave optics, modern physics

Section 3: Chemistry

  • Physical Chemistry: Atomic structure, chemical bonding, thermodynamics, equilibrium, chemical kinetics, electrochemistry
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Periodic table, chemical bonding, s-block, p-block, d-block, and f-block elements, coordination compounds
  • Organic Chemistry: Basic principles, hydrocarbons, alcohols, phenols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, amines, polymers

Exam Pattern and Scoring

  • Mode of Examination: Online (Computer-Based Test)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Total Marks: 300 marks (100 marks per section)
  • Marking Scheme: No negative marking for incorrect answers

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the UPES-EAT exam, candidates must fulfill the following requirements:

  • Must have passed 10+2 or equivalent from a recognized board with a minimum aggregate of 60% marks.
  • Must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects in 10+2.

Preparation Tips

  • Thorough Understanding of Syllabus: Familiarize yourself with the syllabus and exam pattern.
  • Practice with Sample Papers: Solve previous years' question papers and sample papers to get accustomed to the exam format and difficulty level.
  • Time Management: Practice time management during your preparation to ensure you can complete the exam within the given time frame.
  • Conceptual Clarity: Focus on building a strong foundation in the core concepts of Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Stay Calm and Confident: Maintain a positive attitude and believe in your abilities.

List of UPES Schools Accepting UPES-EAT Scores

UPES offers a diverse range of programs across various schools, and the UPES-EAT exam scores are accepted for admission to the following schools:

  • School of Engineering
  • School of Computer Science
  • School of Design
  • School of Law
  • School of Business
  • School of Health Sciences
  • School of Smart Agriculture
