ACET (Actuarial Common Entrance Test) for B.Sc. (Actuarial Science)

Introduction

The Actuarial Common Entrance Test (ACET) is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the Institute of Actuaries of India (IAI) for admission to the B.Sc. (Actuarial Science) program offered by various universities and colleges in India.

Structure of the Exam

The ACET consists of two papers:

  • Paper 1: Mathematics and Statistics (100 marks)
  • Paper 2: English Language and Logical Reasoning (100 marks)

Each paper is of 2 hours duration.

Paper Pattern

Paper 1: Mathematics and Statistics

  • Section A: Mathematics (60 marks)
    • Algebra
    • Calculus
    • Coordinate Geometry
    • Trigonometry
  • Section B: Statistics (40 marks)
    • Descriptive Statistics
    • Inferential Statistics
    • Probability

Paper 2: English Language and Logical Reasoning

  • Section A: English Language (60 marks)
    • Reading Comprehension
    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
  • Section B: Logical Reasoning (40 marks)
    • Verbal Reasoning
    • Analytical Reasoning
    • Critical Thinking

Marking Scheme

  • Each question carries 1 mark.
  • No negative marking.

Colleges that Accept ACET Scores
